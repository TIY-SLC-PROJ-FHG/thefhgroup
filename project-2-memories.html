Goal 1: Gather and safeguard your own important personal records. Vault

Preserving your own personal records gets you off to a good start and helps you protect your valuable information.

Choices

A Gather your own important records.

  1. Locate your birth certificate, religious records, marriage certificate, legal documents, etc.
  2. If you are missing any important records, arrange to get replacements of them.
  3. Watch the video segment (1:25-2:15) for ideas on gathering records.
  4. Learn how to find the treasures in family records that are in your possession.

B Make scans or paper copies of each document to serve as backups.

  1. If you don't have access to a scanner, visit a library or Family History Center to use one; or use a digital camera to make digital copies.
  2. Back up digital copies to external devices (thumb drive, etc.) or to the Internet.
  3. Read these tips on backing up your smartphone photos.

C Store the original documents and backups in separate, secure locations.

  1. Choose a safe, dry place for physical copies.
  2. Explore the CollectionCareTips site for ideas on preserving important photos and documents.
  3. For more ideas on preserving documents and photos, read these articles. |

D Restore damaged photographs when possible.

  1. Learn about restoring photographs in this article.
  2. Get free help restoring or dating your photographs from members of the RootsChat forum.

Goal 2: Browse FamilySearch Memories to see photos and documents for your ancestors.

Seeing what has already been added to FamilySearch Memories will help avoid duplication and perhaps give you ideas for the kinds of images you'll want to add. For an overview, watch this video.

A View Memories for an ancestor.

  1. Find an ancestor's Summary card that has a positive value in the Memories link, and click Memories. You will see any photos, documents, stories, and audio files that have been posted for the ancestor.
  2. For help, see page 1 of this document.
  3. To see the full-size version of a photo, click on it.
  4. To view other photos for the person, without having to return to the person's Memories page, click the Forward or Back buttons at the edges of the screen.
  5. In Landscape view, Portrait view, Fan Chart, or Descendancy View, click an ancestor's name who has a photo showing next to it. Note: Be sure that Portraits have been enabled in the Show menu (see Project 1,

    1, Choice D, step 4).

B Use the People view in Memories to see collections of photos.

  1. In Memories menu at the top of the main FamilySearch window, choose People. An identifying photo is shown for each ancestor who has a photo in Memories.
  2. If you want to limit the number of ancestors on the page, click the Show menu and select a different category. To see descriptions for each category, click the Info button to the right.
  3. To sort the Memories, choose a criteria from the Sort menu.
  4. To search for certain Memories, click the magnifying glass and type the name you want to search for in the Filter field. To return to the full view, click the X at the right of the Filter field.
  5. To see how you are related to a particular ancestor, click View My Relationship at the bottom of a Person square. A vertical pedigree appears, showing the ancestral connection between you and the person.
  6. Click a person's photo to show the photos in the Person page.

C Use the Find feature in Memories to see collections of photos.

  1. In the Memories menu at the top of the main FamilySearch window, choose Find.
  2. Click All, Photos, Stories, or Documents.
  3. Type the name of the item you want to search for, and then click Find. Your search term ("Jones", or "Vienna", or "1850" etc.) will return a list of all FamilySearch Memories items (not just the ones you've added) that have the item in the title or in the contents.
  4. Click an item from the returned list to see its contents.

Goal 3: Organize photos and documents of ancestors for FamilySearch Memories.

This helps you learn about your ancestors' lives in a personal way. Read this article for a glimpse of the possibilities.

A Gather photos and documents you have of your ancestors.

  1. Look at home to see what kinds of family history records are already available.
  2. Record what you know from records and sources easily available, as explained in this video.
  3. Learn about family records and how you can use them. Examples include journals, diaries, letters, photographs, and family bibles. |

B Contact living relatives or others who might have photos or documents for you to add, or might have added them already to Memories.

  1. Watch the video from 2:30-3:30 for tips on getting started.
  2. Click each interesting photo or document in Memories, and then click the "Contributed by" link to see the email address of the contributor (if available).
  3. Compile a contact list from contributors discovered in the previous step.
  4. Read the article for ideas on reaching out to others for photos and documents, and then make the contacts.
  5. Offer to send physical copies of photos or documents to relatives if they would like them.

C Add photos and documents from others to your collection, and catalog them.

  1. Add descriptions for each item in a separate document.
  2. Make notes of missing information and questions you have about any items.
  3. Learn how to add labels to your photographs.
  4. Here are some additional ideas on gathering memories. |

D Use Find-A-Record to see which ancestors are missing portrait photos in Family Tree.

  1. See Project 1, Goal 9 for instructions on using Find-A-Record.
  2. Select Cousins, or Ancestors, or Ancestors and Descendants in the "Who Do You Want to Find" menu.
  3. Select the checkboxes for Sources, Person, and Cleanup to generate a list of ancestors.
  4. In the Research Opportunities list, note the recent ancestors who are missing portrait photos.

Goal 4: Interview living relatives about their memories and life stories. Vault

Capturing family history recollections from living relatives ensures that their legacy can be shared before they pass on.

A Prepare for successful interviews.

  1. Make a list of living people whose stories need to be captured. These people may also be good sources for photos and documents.
  2. Prepare a list of questions in advance that will help your guest express their family history experiences. |
  3. Ensure the questions are respectful and handle any delicate situations carefully.
  4. For tips on successfully capturing stories, read these articles. |

B Hold and record the interview.

  1. Use tips from the videos to help you conduct interviews with relatives. |
  2. Learn what interview techniques to avoid.

C Upload the audio or written interview document to Memories.

  1. Decide which information should remain private (as in a family journal) and which can be uploaded to FamilySearch.
  2. Use the upload process described in Goal 5 for documents, and Goal 9 for audio.

Goal 5: Prepare photos and documents to be uploaded to FamilySearch Memories. Vault

Getting the right photos that are in the right format will save you time and get better results.

A Read the guidelines on FamilySearch Memories for the kinds of items that can be uploaded (to avoid item rejection).

  1. Guidelines are found here.

B Determine which photos are good candidates to upload and which are not.

  1. Exclude any photos that do not meet the Memories guidelines.
  2. Learn about adding memories for a living person.   LANG:   DE   ES   FR   IT   PT   JA  
  3. As you see fit, exclude photos that are duplicates or near-duplicates, that are not well-taken or are damaged, etc.

C Scan any physical photos or documents that you want to upload, or take photos of them with a digital camera.

  1. Add descriptions for each item.(Scanning is preferable for quality; if you don't have a scanner or need help, you can visit a Family History Center.)
  2. Learn the basics of scanning for genealogists.
  3. Improve your digital photography skills with this BYU webinar.

D If necessary, crop or enhance photos or documents after scanning them.

  1. Use FreeOnlinePhotoEditor to enhance digital pictures in your browser.
  2. Try other resources for photo editing.

Goal 6: Add and tag photos in FamilySearch Memories.

Adding photos to Memories makes them available to others, and tagging photos helps to identify photos by their content. Note: Photos can also be uploaded and tagged in the Gallery; see Goal 11 for details.

A Upload your digital photos to Memories.

Note : Before uploading photos, make sure the content fits the approved guidelines found in this article.

  1. In Family Tree, click the name of the ancestor who will have a photo added.
  2. In the Summary card, click Memories.
  3. In the Photos section of the Person page, click Upload Photo.
  4. Navigate to the photo file you want to upload, and then click Open.
  5. Check the "I have read and agree ..." box in the dialog, and then click Continue. The photo will appear in the Gallery for your ancestor, with a "Screening" banner.
  6. Click "Screening" to open the photo.
  7. To add a title for the photo, click Add Title (above the photo to the left), type the text, and then click Save.
  8. To add a description for the photo, type the text in the Description box and click Add Description. Additional options are explained in Choice B below.
  9. For more information on adding photos to your Family Tree, read this article.
  10. If you have any questions about guidelines for photos, read the upload guidelines.

B Tag the persons in each photo to identify who they are.

  1. If your photo is already open in Memories, skip to the next step; otherwise, go to the Person page for an ancestor, click the Memories tab and then the Photos link. Then Click the photo to open it. Note that untagged photos have exclamation points to remind you about tagging.
  2. Click on a person's face in the photo you want to tag.
  3. Drag and size the picture frame that appears until it surrounds the head area in the photo.
  4. Click Save to complete the Tag. Now when you move the mouse over that person in the photo in Memories, the Tag appears with the person's name.
  5. For an overview of tagging photos, read this article.

C Modify or delete tags as needed.

  1. Open a photo in Memories that has one or more tags.
  2. Find the "People in This Photo" box at the right of the page.
  3. In the People in This Photo box, click the ancestor's name whose tag you want to change.
  4. Click the menu choice that matches what you want to do (Memories, Edit Tag, or Remove Tag). If you click Memories, you can detach the photo from the person in Family Tree but still keep the photo in the Gallery.

D Add information about the photo, or delete the photo.

  1. Click Details to do any of the following: view the original photo; rotate the photo left or right; or change the file category from photo to document.
  2. If this photo is associated with an event, click the Event icon and then click Edit to add the date and place the event occurred. Click Save when you are done.
  3. To add a story about the photo, click Stories. Then a) add a title, b) write the story, and c) click Save.
  4. To add the photo to a different Album, click Albums. Then click "Select an album" and choose the Album in the drop-down list.
  5. To delete the photo, click the Delete icon and then click Delete.

E If desired, change the preferred photo for the ancestor.

  1. Read the article for instructions on changing an ancestor's preferred photo in Memories.

Goal 7: Add documents to Memories.

Documents that mention or were written by your ancestor can be valuable sources of information for others. Note: Documents can also be uploaded and tagged in the Gallery; see Goal 11 for details.

A Find one or more documents that you want to upload to FamilySearch.

  1. If the document is in digital form, you are ready to upload; if the document is physical, you need to scan it first to convert to digital. (If you don't have a scanner or need help, ask a friend or visit a Family History Center.)

B Upload the documents to Memories.

  1. Go to the memories tab on a Person page.
  2. In the Documents area, click Upload Document.
  3. Navigate to the document to be uploaded and click Open.
  4. A "Screening" message appears over the document, meaning that FamilySearch is checking guidelines. When the document is approved, it is added to the ancestor's documents in Memories.

C Add people who are mentioned in the document.

  1. Click the document to open it.
  2. Drag and size the box that appears until it surrounds the person's name in the document.
  3. Click Save to complete the tag.

Goal 8: Add stories to Memories. Vault

Stories can be a powerful way to connect you and others to your ancestors. Note: Stories can also be uploaded and tagged in the Gallery; see Goal 11 for details.

A Search for stories that have been uploaded to Memories for your ancestors.

  1. Go to the Ancestors with Memories site.
  2. On the website, blank out the ID box (to start the search from your ID) and click Submit.
  3. Click Sign In at the FamilySearch prompt.
  4. In the results web page that appears, press Ctrl+F and type "text" (without quotes) to highlight the text documents (stories) in Memories for the ancestors in the list.
  5. In FamilySearch, search for one or more ancestors with stories; open their Memories tab; scroll down to the Stories section and browse the stories.

B Find one or more stories that you want to upload to FamilySearch.

  1. Locate stories of interest in your personal collections, including letters, family books, and personal memories. Note: Be aware of copyright issues if you are using a published source for a story.
  2. Reach out to family for stories they might have about your ancestors, using ideas from the documents. |
  3. Examine information in family records to uncover interesting stories to tell. The video has some tips for finding stories that are "hiding" in genealogy records.

C Enhance or create life stories for ancestors.

  1. When possible, add interesting historical details to flesh out an ancestor's life story. |
  2. Watch this BYU Webinar for ideas on creating stories about your ancestors.

D Upload stories to Memories.

Note : Before uploading stories, make sure the content fits the approved guidelines found in this article.

  1. In the Memories page for your ancestor, click the Stories link.
  2. Click the Create Story button.
  3. If this is a brief story, title it and type it in the Story window.
  4. If it's a longer story, write it in a word processor and copy/paste it into the Story window. Important: If you type for over 60 minutes in the Story window, it will close, and your changes will be lost.
  5. If you want to add a new photo to the story, click the Upload Photo link and follow the prompts. To add an existing phot from the Gallery, click Select from Gallery and follow the prompts.
  6. Click Save to upload the story.
  7. For a review of adding stories, read this article.

Goal 9: Write a life sketch.

Life sketches provide a good overview of an ancestor's life. They are not meant to be long and detailed; you can use the Stories and Documents features to capture that kind of information.

A Write a personal sketch for yourself.

  1. Write the sketch in a word processor.
  2. In your Person page, scroll down to Life Sketch and click Open.
  3. Copy and paste your sketch into the Life Sketch area, and then click Save.
  4. For ideas on what to write and how to put your sketch together, see this article.

B Write a personal sketch for an ancestor.

  1. Write the sketch in a word processor and then copy and paste it into the Life Sketch area at the beginning of the ancestor's Person page; or type it directly into the Life Sketch area.
  2. For ideas on writing a life sketch for an ancestor, read these documents. |
  3. For ideas on creating a chronology for an ancestor, read this article.

Goal 10: Gather audio files and add them to Memories.

Audio recordings of recent ancestors speaking can be priceless additions to your family history. Note: You can also record audio in an interview with a relative and upload it later. See Goal 4 for details about interviewing living relatives. Also, audio files can be uploaded and tagged in the Gallery; see Goal 11 for details.

A Find one or more audio files that you want to upload to Memories.

  1. Locate existing audio files of interest in your personal collection, (.MP3 or .M4A format, up to 15MB each). If you have cassette tapes with recordings you want to upload, see Goal C below.
  2. Reach out to family for audio files they might have about your ancestors.

B Upload the audio files to Memories.

  1. In the Person page, scroll down to the Audio section and click Upload Audio.
  2. Navigate to the audio file, and click Open. The file appears in the Audio section.
  3. If you want to test the sound, click the Play button for the file.
  4. Scroll down to the audio section and find the file you added.
  5. Click Details to open the details page and play the audio file.
  6. To add a title for the photo, click the Edit Title link above the file name, type the text, and click Save.
  7. To add a description for the audio, type the text in the Description box and click Add Description.
  8. Type a name in the "Who is in this audio?" field.
  9. To associate this audio with an event, a) click the Event icon; b) click Edit; c) fill in the date and place; and d) click Save.
  10. For tips on adding audio, refer to the article.   LANG:   ES  
  11. To delete an audio file, a) click the Delete icon; b) click Delete.

C Convert cassette tape recordings to digital so they can be uploaded.

  1. Download a sound converter program onto your computer, such as Audacity for PC or Macs. (This is a shareware product with a free trial.)

D Get help with converting recordings.

  1. Go to one of the larger Family History Centers, such as the Salt Lake, Riverton, or Sandy Granite centers, and have them help you convert cassettes to digital files.
  2. For recording types not available at a Family History Center, use the services at a professional audio or camera store.

Goal 11: Use the Gallery feature in Memories.

Using the Gallery is an efficient way to organize and manage your photos, documents, etc.

A Find or add items in the Gallery.

  1. In the Memories menu at the top of the FamilySearch screen, choose Gallery.
  2. To add an item to the Gallery, drag it into the main Gallery window; OR, click the green Plus button in the window to open a file upload dialog.
  3. If you want to choose only a certain type of item to display, click the icon for Photos, Stories, Documents, or Audio.
  4. To sort the Memories, choose a criteria from the Arrange menu.
  5. To use a list view for the Memories, click the List View icon (next to the Search magnifying glass).
  6. To search for certain Memories, click the magnifying glass and type the name you want to search for in the Filter field. To return to the full view, click the X at the right of the Filter field.

B Select an action for one or more items in the Gallery.

  1. Click the checkmark for one or more items in the Gallery. The Actions ribbon appears near the top of the Memories window.
  2. Click Actions, or right-click a selected item, to display the Actions menu. Note : If you have multiple items selected, or if you have selected a document or story, the Actions menu choices are limited.
  3. Choose an Action: Open in New Window, Rotate Left (rotates a photo 90 degrees to the left), Rotate Right (rotates a photo 90 degrees to the right), Download (copies the item to your local device), Add to Album, Change to Photo or Document (converts a photo to a document or vice versa), Move to My Archive, Delete.
  4. To add a comment to an item, click the item, click the "Add a comment" box, type the comment, and click Add Comment. A conversation icon appears in the Gallery on each item that has a comment.

C Create a new photo album.

  1. In the left pane, under Albums, click New Album.
  2. Type the name of the album and click Save.

D Add photos to existing Albums.

  1. In the Gallery, hover over a Memory you want to add to the Album you created. You will see a checkmark in the upper-right corner of the Memory.
  2. Click the checkmark for the Memory to highlight it.
  3. Click the checkmarks for any other Memories you want to add to the Album. Note : You can press Shift and click the last Memory in a group to select all Memories in between. To unselect, click the X at the right of the Option bar on top. To show only a certain type of Memories, click its icon in the Show bar.
  4. Drag the selected Memories into the desired Album in the left pane; OR
    Click Add to Album; click Select the Album; and click Add to Album. Note : You can use the default MyFavorites album as well.

E Edit or delete an Album.

  1. Click the Album name in the left pane.
  2. To change the Album title, click Edit Title, type the name, and click Save.
  3. To change the Album description, click Add Description (or Edit Description), type the name, and click Save.
  4. To delete an Album, click the Delete Album link and then click Delete Album.
